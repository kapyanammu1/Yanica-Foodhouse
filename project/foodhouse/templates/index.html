TAPSILOG

Tapsilog is a popular Filipino breakfast dish consisting of three main components: Tapa (marinated beef), sinangag (garlic fried rice), and itlog (fried egg).

It originated from the combination of the words "tapa," "sinangag," and "itlog." Tapa is typically thinly sliced beef, marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, and other spices, then fried until tender and flavorful.

CHICKSILOG

Chickilog is a variation of the popular Filipino breakfast dish, replacing the meat component with chicken.

It typically consists of chicken tocino (sweet cured chicken), sinangag (garlic fried rice), and itlog (fried egg). Chicken tocino is made by marinating chicken meat in a sweet and savory sauce, often made with ingredients like pineapple juice, soy sauce, garlic, and sugar.

HOTSILOG

Hotsilog is a breakfast dish that features hotdog (or "hot") as the main protein component, combined with sinangag (garlic fried rice) and itlog (fried egg).

he hotdogs used in hotsilog are typically long, thin, and slightly sweet in flavor. They are commonly served sliced lengthwise and pan-fried until slightly charred.

PORKSILOG

Porksilog is a classic Filipino breakfast dish comprising pork, sinangag (garlic fried rice), and itlog (fried egg).

The pork component is typically represented by pork tocino, which is marinated in a sweet and savory mixture before being pan-fried until caramelized and tender. The combination of the flavorful pork, fragrant garlic fried rice, and a sunny-side-up fried egg makes porksilog a satisfying and delicious morning meal option.
